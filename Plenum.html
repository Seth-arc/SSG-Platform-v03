How it works

Four movements, one session.

Plenum runs a complete exercise from the first brief to the after-action review — structured, observable, and repeatable.

01

Convene

Stand up a scenario in minutes. Assign roles and cells, distribute read-ahead briefs, and seat every participant — in the room or remote.

02

Deliberate

A white cell drives the scenario forward, releasing intelligence and injects on a controlled clock while teams negotiate, plan, and move.

03

Decide

Every choice is logged against a ticking decision window. No take-backs — only consequences that ripple into the next move.

04

Debrief

Replay the timeline, surface what each cell knew and when, and turn the session into a structured, honest after-action review.

Capabilities

The control room for a serious exercise.

Everything a facilitator needs to design, run, and study a high-consequence simulation — from one console.

Roles & cells

Build Blue, Red, and White cells, assign roles, and control exactly what each participant can see and do.

White-cell control

Drive the scenario, release injects, adjudicate moves, and steer tempo in real time from a single facilitator view.

Controlled intelligence

Release documents, signals, and media on a timeline — or triggered by player moves — so information arrives the way it would in the real world.

Decision capture

Every decision, rationale, and timestamp is recorded automatically and tied to the move it belongs to.

Notetaker & transcript

Built-in observers capture deliberation and table-talk without breaking the room's flow.

Research mode & analytics

Run the same scenario across cohorts, compare outcomes, and export the data for teaching or formal research.

In session

Pressure you can watch unfold.

The facilitator sees the whole board — the clock, the cells, and every inject — while players see only what their role would know.

  • A controlled clock. Moves advance on a tempo you set — or pause for adjudication when the room needs it.
  • Asymmetric information. Blue, Red, and White each hold a different picture of the same crisis.
  • Injects on cue. Drop a new report, a leaked signal, or a press query at exactly the right moment.
  • A complete record. When the session ends, the timeline is already written.
