A plenum is the full assembly in session — every voice present, the chamber complete. It is also a space entirely filled by a medium: a room thick with consequence. The name holds both meanings deliberately.

ple·num · /ˈplē-nəm/ · noun

1. An assembly of all members of a deliberative body, convened to decide.

2. A space wholly filled — a charged, active field with no empty part.

The mark draws the room itself: the full body seated around the table, every voice turned toward the single matter at its center.
